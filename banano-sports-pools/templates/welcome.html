How Do The Pools Work?

Place deposits with Banano on the teams you think will win their game! Your deposit will be placed in a pool in support of that team. If your team wins, you will receive your initial deposit back plus your percent of the winning pool from deposits placed on the opposing team. If your team loses, well, your deposit will be split among the winners.

For example, let's say BUF and LAR are playing each other. You deposit 10 BAN on BUF. The total amount deposited on BUF is 100 BAN. The total amount deposited on LAR is 80 BAN. Since your deposit makes up 10% of BUF's pool, if BUF wins, you will receive your deposit (10 BAN) plus 10% of LAR's pool, or in this case, 8 BAN. In the case of a tie, all BAN will be returned. If no deposits are made on one side of the matchup, then all BAN will be returned to their original owners. Deposits lock at the time of kickoff. You can make multiple deposits on a matchup, and even on both sides, however, a single BAN Address must be used.

To make your deposits more fair and informed, the Vegas Odds and implied win probability for each game are provided. If you believe in these probabilities, then the percent of BAN in each team's pool should be close to these percentages. Further, if you check the box called "Recommend Teams/Games", we will recommend games and teams whose pools are imbalanced according to the Vegas Odds.

There currently is NO HOUSE FEE, but this might change in the future. All payouts go out hourly on the 15 and are automated and transparent.

What's new in v2.x?

While the front end aspects of the application are minor, the entire backend and database have been rebuilt. The purpose of this was multi-faceted. As I scaled to multiple sports, I ran into problems with how the database kept track of deposits, as well as had to use multiple BAN addresses for payments. It is hoped that the new infrastructure will facilitate an easier time of scaling out to other sports and use the singular address that is already saved to many address books.

Sure, great, but what cool stuff have you added?

AUTOMATION. Automation everywhere.

The plans in the works for v2.x include adding other sports in a scalable, sustainable way, with continued expansion for the automation of payouts and metadata refreshes.

What the fork is BANANO?

BANANO was forked in April 2018 from NANO. BANANO offers instant, feeless and rich in potassium üçå transactions, thanks to the fact that BANANO developers (several of them having being involved in NANO itself) have kept big portions of the original code unchanged to keep cross-chain compatibility between existing code libraries. However, they have fined-tuned some parameters, such as Proof of Work requirements and currency units. While the focus for now is on having an ongoing free and fair distribution, BANANO is also experimenting with feature additions such as a privacy layer (Camo BANANO), on-chain messaging (MonkeyTalks) and more. In context of distribution, we aim to use our meanwhile ready-to-strike infrastructure with easy-to-use mobile wallets (Kalium) and tipbots on several major social media platforms to onboard normies and crypto-noobs who have no idea yet what a cryptocurrency is. We also might do IRL airdrops at some point. Of note, key here is to make the start with crypto as easy as possible, use a fun attitude and gamification to get new users started without all the usual hassle, and then educate them to handle crypto in general in a responsible way.
